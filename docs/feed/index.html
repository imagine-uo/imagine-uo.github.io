The programme of this event consisted in 4 keynote talks and 8 non-parallel sessions, each of which was divided in two parts. In the first part, the researchers gave a short presentation (either 4 or 9 minutes) about their works, whereas in the second one, there was one-hour poster session dedicated to discussing the topic. David Nieto-Barba, Enrique Miranda and Marina Iturrate-Bobes attended this conference. 

David Nieto-Barba presented his work entitled “Distorting lower probabilities using common distortion models” done in collaboration with Ignacio Montes and Enrique Miranda on 15 July. He discussed the generalisation of several prominent distortion models to the case of an initially imprecise model. More concretely, imprecise extensions of the Vertical Barrier Models (which include the Linear Vacuous and Pari-Mutuel Model, as well as the Total Variation Model) were introduced; and a number of desirable properties were analysed in this context, for the purpose of enhancing robustness of already imprecise models  His poster was awarded with the 3rd Price Poster Award. 

Enrique Miranda’s work (in collaboration with Ignacio Montes) was entitled “On the closure of aggregation rules for imprecise probabilities”. He gave his presentation on 15 July, in which he discussed the problem of aggregating several imprecise probability models using different aggregation rules, investigating whether the aggregation rules are closed in the sense that the aggregated model belongs to the same family as the inputs. Moreover, he was elected as the President of the Society for Imprecise Probabilities for the period 2025-2027. 

On 17 July, Marina Iturrate-Bobes presented her work “How do normality tests behave for rounded data?” in collaboration with Ignacio Montes and Raúl Pérez-Fernández. In this contribution, she tackled this topic from the perspective of random sets, which were used as a model to explain how the most extended normality tests (Anderson-Darling, Lilliefors and Shapiro -Wilk) are affected when the data is rounded. For this purpose, she explored the power of the three tests and their respective acceptance, indecision and rejection regions.  

This conference format provided participants with the opportunity to hold discussions and exchange ideas. The poster discussion sessions of David, Enrique and Marina were all well attended, which allowed them to have quite interesting discussions with other researchers. Furthermore, the well-organized social program was also enriching. From here, we extend our gratitude to the organizers and look forward to participating in the next ISIPTA Conference. 

]]> New published paper at IJUFKS! http://imagine.local/2025/09/30/new-published-paper-at-ijufks/ Tue, 30 Sep 2025 07:29:02 +0000 http://imagine.local/?p=759 We are pleased to announce that our paper

The Total Variation Distance for Comparing Non-Additive Measures

has been accepted for publication in the International Journal of Uncertainty, Fuzziness and Knowledge-Based Systems. 

This paper, written by David Nieto-Barba, Enrique Miranda and Ignacio Montes, explores the comparison of non-additive probabilities, with the Total Variation distance between probability measures as baseline. The first proposal extends this distance  by taking the minimum Total Variation distance among the pairs of probability measures dominating the respective non-additive measures, while the second one replaces the minimum by a maximum. In our last approach, we consider the minimum of the supremum distances instead.

The preliminary Section 2 is followed by the analysis of each of these approaches in the respective Sections 3, 4 and 5. More concretely, for each proposal we investigate the distance-like properties fulfilled, computational simplifications in terms of the boundary or the extreme points of the set of dominating probabilities, alternative expressions in terms of the values of the non-additive measures and their application to the distortion of non additive measures.

In doing so, we demonstrate that neither of the extensions considered is a distance and, while the computation of the first a third approaches reduces to the boundary of the sets of dominating probabilities, the third one may be simply computed in terms of the extreme points. Moreover, we find sufficent conditions allowing us to express the minimum and maximum Total Variation distances and subsequent distortions directly in terms of the values of the non-additive measures.

To conclude, the Section 7 proves the connection of the distortions by the minimum distances proposals with the strong and weak cores in coalition game theory.

]]> EUSFLAT 2025 Report http://imagine.local/2025/08/08/eusflat-2025-report/ Fri, 08 Aug 2025 18:36:50 +0000 http://imagine.local/?p=811 On 21-25 July 2025, the 14th Conference of the European Society for Fuzzy Logic and Technology, EUSFLAT 2025, was held in the House of Science, University of Latvia, Riga (Latvia). This conference was attended by 152 participants and consisted of 7 plenary talks and 14 special sessions. Raúl Pérez-Fernández and Marina Iturrate-Bobes were the representatives of IMAGINE at the conference.

Raúl Pérez-Fernández was in charge of organizing the special session “New contexts in Aggregation Theory”, in collaboration with Bernard De Baets from Ghent University. The main topic of the session was aggregation theory, with a special focus on extending aggregation methods beyond the classical approaches to richer data structures such as compositional, directional, functional, image, multivariate, ranking and string data.  11 authors presented their works in this special session, which was divided in two sessions.

In addition to organizing a special session, Rául Pérez-Fernández presented his work “An Axiomatic Study of the Properties Satisfied by Methods for Ranking Elements of a Poset”, in collaboration with Ignacio Montes and Bernard De Baets. During his presentation, he discussed some axiomatic properties that a method for aggregating elements in a poset may satisfy and analyzed which of these properties are satisfied by some common methods used for this purpose. Marina Iturrate-Bobes also presented her work “Goodness-of-Fit tests to Location-Scale Families Based on OWA functions” (in colaboration with Ignacio Montes and Raúl Pérez-Fernández). During her talk, she introduced a family of skewness coefficients based on OWA functions (more specifically, on a linear combination of quantiles). She presented its main properties as well as its sample version and asymptotic distribution.  These coefficients were later applied to goodness-of-fit tests to location-scale families. Furthermore, she was awarded with an EUSFLAT student grant to attend the conference. 

It must be remarked that Raúl Pérez-Fernández was a coauthor of another work: “Construction of Uninorms on Bounded Lattices: Augmenting the flexibility of Choice”, done in collaboration with Ander Goñi-Medrano and Marisol Gómez-Fernández, from the Public University of Navarra and the Navarra Artificial Intelligence Research Center (NAIR Center).

The conference was a highly enriching and interesting event, with excellent organization throughout. It did not only offer a strong and diverse scientific program, where researchers could present and discuss their latest works, but also included a carefully planed social program.

From here, we would like to thank the organizers for a wonderful conference and look forward to the next EUSFLAT Conference.

]]> New published paper! http://imagine.local/2023/10/28/what-ive-learned-from-road-trips/ Sat, 28 Oct 2023 15:45:40 +0000 http://imagine.local/?p=15 We are happy to announce that our paper entitled:

Inner approximations of coherent lower probabilities and their application to decision making problems

has been accepted for publication in the Annals of Operations Research. This paper was done by Enrique Miranda and Ignacio Montes in collaboration with Andrés Presa, a young mathematician and physicist who is currently taking a master course on astrophysics in the Leiden University (The Netherlands). This paper was part of his final Bachellor’s project on mathematics.

In this paper we formalise the notion of inner approximation of a coherent lower probability, which is a another coherent lower probability whose credal set is more precise. Hence, we go in the opposite direction than in our previous papers about outer approximations. The paper is structured as follows:

  • After detailing some preliminary concepts and notation in Section 2, in Section 3 we formalise the notion of inner approximation and we show how to compute optimal 2- or complete-monotone inner approximations by solving (linear or quadratic) opmisation problems. We also detail the similarities and differences between inner and outer approximations.
  • In Section 4 we analyse the problem of inner approximating a coherent lower probability by a distortion model such as the linear vacuous, pari mutuel or total variation models. Somehow surprising, we show that the problem of finding an optimal inner approximation in these families is equivalent to the problem of finding the centroid of a credal set.
  • In Section 5 we explain the common setting of a decision making problem under uncertainty and we show how inner and outer approximations can be used to simplify the computation and how they behave for the common criteria in the IP literature (gamma-maximin and maximax, maximality, interval dominance and E-admissibility).
  • Finally, in Section 6 we illustrate how to apply the results in Section 5 in an example.

If you want to take a look at the paper, you can find it here, in the AOR website, where it available as open access.
